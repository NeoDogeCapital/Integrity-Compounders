## The Business
Applied Materials is the largest semiconductor equipment manufacturer globally, providing the machinery and software that fabricate chips. The company holds dominant positions in deposition, etch, and metrology tools—critical equipment used in every major fab worldwide. AMAT generates revenue through equipment sales (hardware), with recurring revenue streams from service contracts, spare parts, and process upgrades. This business model creates sticky customer relationships with foundries, logic manufacturers, and memory producers who depend on AMAT's technology to achieve yield improvements and node transitions.

The company's competitive advantage stems from decades of process knowledge embedded in its tools and a scale advantage that competitors struggle to match. TSMC, Samsung, and Intel all rely heavily on AMAT equipment for advanced node production. The service segment (roughly 30% of revenue) provides high-margin recurring revenue with strong visibility. AMAT's technology leadership in areas like selective deposition and advanced patterning creates switching costs, while its global service network and installed base of over 50,000 systems make displacement difficult for smaller competitors like Lam Research or Tokyo Electron.
