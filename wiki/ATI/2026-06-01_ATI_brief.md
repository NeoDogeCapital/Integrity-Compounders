## The Business
ATI Inc. manufactures specialty materials and components for the aerospace and defense industry, focusing on advanced titanium and nickel-based alloys, precision forgings, and engineered materials. The company operates through two primary segments: High Performance Materials & Components (HPMC), which produces specialty alloys and forgings for jet engines and airframes, and Advanced Alloys & Solutions (AA&S), which serves broader industrial markets including oil & gas and medical. ATI's competitive advantage stems from its technical expertise in producing mission-critical materials that meet stringent aerospace certifications, along with long-term supply agreements with major OEMs like Boeing, Airbus, and engine manufacturers.

The economics are straightforward: ATI sells into multi-year aerospace build cycles where switching costs are prohibitive once materials are certified for specific aircraft platforms. Operating margin of 15.0% and ROIC of 13.9% reflect a business with decent returns, though not exceptional given the technical barriers to entry. The company's position as one of few qualified suppliers for certain titanium alloys provides pricing power during upcycles, but aerospace exposure creates significant cyclicality and working capital intensity.
