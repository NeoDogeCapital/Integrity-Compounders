## The Business

Caterpillar is the global leader in construction and mining equipment, diesel and natural gas engines, industrial gas turbines, and diesel-electric locomotives. The company operates through four segments: Construction Industries (earthmoving and compact equipment), Resource Industries (mining and heavy construction equipment), Energy & Transportation (engines and turbines for power generation and industrial applications), and Financial Products (financing to customers and dealers). With a 25% share of the global heavy equipment market, CAT's competitive moat stems from its unmatched dealer network of 150+ independent dealers in 190+ countries, providing parts availability and service that competitors cannot replicate.

The business model generates revenue through equipment sales (65% of revenue), aftermarket parts and service (30%), and financial services (5%). Parts revenue carries 35-40% margins versus 15-20% on new equipment, creating a flywheel where installed base expansion drives high-margin recurring revenue. The dealer network acts as both distribution and customer relationship manager, reducing CAT's direct selling costs while ensuring equipment uptime—the critical variable for mining and construction customers. Brand loyalty in heavy equipment is exceptionally high due to switching costs, operator familiarity, and the multi-decade service relationship.
