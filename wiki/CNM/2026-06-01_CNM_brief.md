## The Business

Core & Main is the largest distributor of water, wastewater, storm drainage, and fire protection products in the United States. The company operates approximately 300+ branches serving municipal, non-residential, and residential end markets, selling pipes, valves, fittings, and ancillary infrastructure products. CNM generates revenue through local branch distribution with a roughly 50/50 split between municipal water infrastructure projects and private construction activity. The business model is capital-light with minimal manufacturing exposure—CNM sources from third-party suppliers and provides logistical value through localized inventory placement and technical expertise.

The competitive advantage is fragmented but defensible. Scale matters in this industry through purchasing leverage, branch density, and the ability to stock a deep SKU base that smaller regional distributors cannot match. CNM's national footprint provides access to large municipal contracts and preferred vendor relationships. However, switching costs are modest, and the business is fundamentally a volume game leveraged to construction activity and infrastructure spending cycles. ROIC of 10.6% is respectable for distribution but not exceptional, reflecting the commoditized nature of many product lines and limited pricing power outside demand surges.
