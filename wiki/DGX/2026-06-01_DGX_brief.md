## The Business

Quest Diagnostics is the largest clinical laboratory testing company in the United States, operating approximately 2,200 patient service centers and processing roughly 500,000 requisitions daily. The company generates revenue primarily through diagnostic information services—blood work, genetic testing, drug monitoring, anatomic pathology—serving physicians, hospitals, employers, and government programs. Quest operates in a duopoly with LabCorp, benefiting from scale advantages in negotiating with payers, distributing fixed costs across massive test volumes, and maintaining a national footprint that regional competitors cannot replicate.

The competitive moat here is structural but not insurmountable. Switching costs exist through physician workflow integration and insurance network positioning, while capital requirements and regulatory complexity create barriers to entry. However, Quest faces persistent pricing pressure from commercial payers, growing competition from hospital-owned labs performing more tests in-house, and technology disruption from at-home testing and digital diagnostics. The 8.6% ROIC suggests adequate but unexceptional returns on capital—this is a toll-road business where volume leverage matters more than pricing power.
