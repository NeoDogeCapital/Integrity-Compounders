## The Business

Freeport-McMoRan is one of the world's largest copper producers, operating a portfolio of long-life, geographically diverse mining assets primarily focused on copper, gold, and molybdenum. The company's flagship operations include the Grasberg minerals district in Indonesia (one of the world's largest copper and gold deposits), extensive operations in North and South America including Morenci and Cerro Verde, and significant molybdenum production. FCX essentially sells into global commodity markets where pricing is set by supply-demand dynamics, with copper representing roughly 85% of revenue and benefiting from structural demand tailwinds in electrification, renewable energy, and data center buildouts.

The company's competitive advantage stems from its low-cost, Tier 1 asset base with decades of reserve life, operational scale that provides cost advantages, and geographic diversification that mitigates country-specific risk. FCX has transitioned from its leveraged 2015-2016 period to a disciplined capital allocator with improving returns. However, this remains a commodity business exposed to copper price volatility, and the company competes against other majors like BHP, Rio Tinto, and Southern Copper on cost curve positioning rather than pricing power.
