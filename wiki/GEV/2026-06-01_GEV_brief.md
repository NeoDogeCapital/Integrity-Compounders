## The Business

GE Vernova is the global energy infrastructure spin-off from General Electric, comprising three core segments: Power (gas turbines, steam turbines, generators), Wind (onshore and offshore turbines), and Electrification (grids, software, power conversion). The company generates revenue through equipment sales, long-term service agreements (a sticky, high-margin business), and grid modernization solutions. This portfolio positions GEV at the intersection of traditional baseload power generation and renewable energy transition, serving utilities, independent power producers, and industrial customers across 100+ countries.

The competitive moat is modest but real. GEV holds a meaningful installed base of gas turbines and wind turbines that require decades of servicing—creating a captive annuity stream. The Power segment benefits from expertise in combined-cycle efficiency and hydrogen-ready turbines, while Electrification plays into grid stability needs as intermittent renewables scale. However, the Wind segment faces margin pressure from project delays and supply chain volatility, and the company competes against Siemens Energy, Vestas, and emerging Chinese manufacturers. The servicing annuity provides ballast, but equipment sales remain cyclical and capital-intensive.
