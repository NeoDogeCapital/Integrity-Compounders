## The Business

Lear Corporation is a Tier 1 automotive supplier with two core divisions: Seating ($15B+ annual revenue) and E-Systems ($6B+). The Seating business manufactures complete seat systems and components for global OEMs, competing primarily on engineering integration, just-in-time delivery, and weight reduction capabilities. E-Systems produces electrical distribution systems, connection systems, and increasingly sophisticated electronics architectures as vehicles electrify. Lear holds the #2 position globally in automotive seating behind Adient and a top-three position in electrical architectures, with significant content on Ford F-Series, GM crossovers, and BMW platforms.

The competitive moat here is narrow but real. Seating is a scale game with 5-7% operating margins at best, where Lear's global footprint and co-location strategy with OEM assembly plants create switching costs. E-Systems offers better economics (8-10% margins) and structural tailwinds as electrical content per vehicle rises with electrification, ADAS, and connectivity. The company's position is defensible within existing platforms due to integration complexity, but it faces intense price-down pressure annually and limited pricing power. ROIC of 10.4% sits barely above cost of capital, reflecting the capital-intensive, commoditized nature of auto supply.
