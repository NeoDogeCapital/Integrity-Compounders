## The Business

Eli Lilly is a global pharmaceutical company focused on discovering, developing, manufacturing, and selling human pharmaceuticals. The company operates across several therapeutic areas including diabetes, oncology, immunology, and neuroscience. Its business model centers on developing patent-protected drugs that command premium pricing during exclusivity periods, then transitioning revenue to newer products as patents expire. Current portfolio strength comes from GLP-1 receptor agonists (Mounjaro for diabetes, Zepbound for obesity), oncology assets, and established diabetes franchises like Trulicity.

LLY's competitive advantage historically rested on R&D productivity and commercial execution in specialty markets. The company demonstrated exceptional operating leverage with 47.3% operating margins and 35.3% ROIC, reflecting pricing power and efficient manufacturing scale. However, pharmaceuticals is an inherently cyclical innovation business—competitive advantage only persists through continuous pipeline replenishment. The current market cap of $985B prices in sustained dominance in the obesity market, which faces increasing competition from Novo Nordisk and emerging oral alternatives.
