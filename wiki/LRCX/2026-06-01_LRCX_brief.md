## The Business

Lam Research designs, manufactures, and services semiconductor wafer fabrication equipment. The company holds dominant positions in etch (wet and dry) and deposition (CVD, ALD, PVD) systems—critical process steps in chip manufacturing. These tools enable chipmakers to pattern and build nanoscale circuit structures on silicon wafers. LRCX generates revenue through equipment sales (70-75% of mix), aftermarket services, and spare parts, with gross margins typically in the mid-40s range. The installed base model creates recurring revenue streams as customers upgrade processes and maintain existing tools.

The competitive moat rests on process complexity and switching costs. As logic and memory manufacturers push toward sub-3nm nodes and advanced 3D architectures (GAA, DRAM stacking), etch and deposition become more mission-critical. LRCX's technology leadership in atomic-layer precision and chamber design creates high customer dependency. The duopoly structure with Applied Materials in most categories limits pricing pressure, while the capital intensity required for R&D (14-15% of sales) acts as a structural barrier. Customer concentration is high—top five customers drive 60%+ of revenue—but these relationships span decades with deep co-development arrangements.
